APPA Website Rebuild

Skills: HTML, CSS, Miro, UX/UI, information architecture

The Task

During my internship at APPA Leadership in Education Facilities, I was tasked with helping build a new organizational website that balanced professional branding with user-friendly navigation. The goal was to design a platform that could both support APPA’s day-to-day needs and remain sustainable for long-term maintenance.

The Process

I used Novi AMS’s web builder to establish the navigation and overarching layout, then customized individual pages through HTML and applied global styling with CSS. To plan and structure the project, I worked with Miro for large-scale organization and incorporated UX/UI design principles to ensure clarity and accessibility. Along the way, I faced challenges such as balancing visually engaging page designs with the need for simplicity, as well as avoiding high-maintenance features that could burden future administrators.

The Results

The final website successfully provided APPA with a professional, easy-to-use digital presence. Beyond the technical skills, I gained experience tailoring design choices to a specific audience, prioritizing usability over personal preference, and building low-maintenance solutions that save organizations time and effort. This project strengthened my abilities in front-end development, UX/UI design, and strategic problem-solving.

Mobile Game (Unity) — “Flower With a Stick”

Skills: C#, Unity, 2D art & animation, gameplay design, user testing, MVP scope management

The Task

Independently, I designed and developed a 2D arcade-style mobile game in Unity using C#. The objective was to create an engaging, polished experience where players control a plant fighting off waves of bugs, while learning the full development pipeline for mobile gaming.

The Process

I created sprites, animations, and gameplay mechanics while integrating user feedback to refine the player experience. Initially, I used a virtual joystick system that felt intuitive to me but proved frustrating for others during testing. Accepting this feedback, I rebuilt the control system from scratch, ultimately designing a swipe-based mechanic inspired by popular games like Fruit Ninja and Subway Surfers. I also scaled back scope by focusing on delivering a minimum viable product (MVP), rather than chasing dozens of levels, enemies, and cosmetics that could have led to burnout.

The Results

The game is near completion and scheduled for release soon. Through this project, I strengthened my skills in C#, Unity, and game design while learning valuable lessons in scope management, user-centered design, and iterative development. The experience demonstrated my ability to accept feedback, pivot strategically, and deliver a product that balances creativity with playability.
